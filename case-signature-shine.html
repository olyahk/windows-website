Signature Shine LLC — Case Study

Service & UX design for a local cleaning brand in Cedar Falls, IA
Role: Founder • UX • Brand Timeline: 2025–Present Tools: Figma, Google Forms, Sheets

Overview

Signature Shine LLC is a residential and commercial cleaning service I founded in Cedar Falls, Iowa. As a student and an employee, I wanted to experience entrepreneurship first‑hand. I studied UX approach to learn what makes a brand/business/interface succeed. I though of something that was realistic, hands-on, and could have had a high demand. The goal was to create a service experience that felt personal, reliable, and gratifying. To bring it to life, I applied what I’ve learned so far — UX research, design thinking, and targeted marketing experiments.

Problem

Local competitors struggled with consistency of service, staff retention, and unclear communication (what’s included, when they arrive, how to prepare). It was not the problem that different people were coming to clean, it was the the service they provided was not consistent. This distroyed trust. In interviews, many women in households felt they “should be able to do it themselves,” despite being overwhelmed by responsibilities, which was leading to guilt.

Research

  • Interviewed 10+ potential customers about needs, barriers, and definitions of “quality.”
  • Created a personal clear checklist and early bookings messaging system to set expectations.
  • Reviewed competitor sites/ads to benchmark messaging, pricing clarity, and service menus.
  • Gathered feedback right after the service and one week post-survey.

Design & Strategy

Brand & Visuals. Built a friendly, trustworthy identity (blues, rounded type, simple icons). Designed leave‑behind cards and message templates to reinforce consistency.

Service Journey. Mapped the end‑to‑end flow: inquiry → quote → day‑of checklist → booking → reminders → follow‑up. Removed ambiguity at each step.

Communication System. Standardized confirmations, reminders, and post‑service follow‑ups. Added a prep checklist and arrival window to reduce uncertainty.

A/B Marketing. Tested direct vs. general messaging. Direct outreach, with introduction and getting to know the customer achieved way higher engagement than flyers; direct pricing copy converted faster for quotes.

Implementation

  • Built simple CRM in Sheets with status tags (new, quoted, scheduled, completed, follow‑up).
  • Created feedback form (Google Forms) with NPS + open comments; logged outcomes to inform changes.
  • Documented SOPs for staff onboarding, quality checks, and rework handling.

Outcomes

  • 7+ repeat clients in first 3 months; built predictable weekly pipeline.
  • ~40% higher quality service after updating personal checklist and gathering feedback.
  • Established a measurable feedback loop (NPS + comments) to guide service improvements.

What I Learned

UX isn’t limited to screens. Every message, arrival window, and follow‑up shapes the user experience. By applying research and simple systems, I improved trust and reliability — and saw direct business impact.
